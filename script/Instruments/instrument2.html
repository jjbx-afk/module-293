Quanun

The qanun is a trapezoidal zither with 72–81 strings, played using plectrums attached to the fingers. Its sound is crystalline and ethereal, capable of evoking cascading waterfalls or shimmering light. Often the centerpiece of traditional ensembles, the qanun’s virtuosity captures the imagination.

The qanun can adjust its tuning mid-performance using levers, making it an ideal instrument for traversing maqamat with rapid modulation. It excels in maqamat like Maqam Hijaz (mystical and exotic) and Maqam Nahawand (romantic and melancholic).
